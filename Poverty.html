POVERTY IN SOMALIA

Somalia is the POOREST country in the World and one of the least developed countries in the world. The poverty rate in Somalia is currently 73 percent, Only about 27 percent of people in this country can afford to feed themselves, with a 4.98% overall happiness rating out of 10. Over 60% of Somalia youths have intentions to leave the country for better livelihood opportunities. Less than 31% of the population in this country has access to clean water and 23% of the whole population has access to improved sanitation facility while the remaining 77% don’t. The lack of hygiene in this country makes people get so many infection and diseases that leads to so many deaths. According to World Health Organization (WHO), lower respiratory infections is still the leading cause of deaths in Somalia, followed by diarrhoeal diseases, measles, malnutrition, tuberculosis, meningitis and maternal conditions. Somalia has a very poor health care and most people can’t really afford it because most of the health care in Somalia is private.

There are so many causes of poverty in this country, the main cause is unemployment; Overall the rate of unemployed people 15 to 64 in this country is 54% while the rate of youth 14 to 29 is 67%. Women lose more with a 74% of unemployment compared to men at 61%. The rate of unemployment has increased by 7% between 2002 and 2019.

Other causes of poverty in Somalia;
1. Lack of viable education
2. Clan and cultural prejudices. Young women are mostly forced to take on traditional occupations due to entrenched traditional gender roles because they mostly believe men are better than women in all ways.
3. A small formal sector that cannot fully accommodate the country’s population.
4. The country cannot attract foreign investments because of the security situation in the country.

Less than 31% of the population has access to clean water and 23% has access to improved sanitation facility.

POVERTY IN LIBERIA

Liberia is one of the poorest countries in the world, with an extremely underdeveloped economy, largely due to the first Civil War. The civil war destroyed much of Liberia's economy. An estimated 64 percent of Liberians live below the poverty line in which 18 percent live in extreme poverty. 2.70 of the population is unemployed. Liberia is a low-income country that depends heavily on foreign assistance, this country depends on imports, which does not help with its agricultural markets already being poorly. This country lives on less than $1 a day.
